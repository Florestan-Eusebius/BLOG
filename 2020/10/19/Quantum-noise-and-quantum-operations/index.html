Environment and quantum operations

A quantum operation of a principal system interacting with environment is defined as

An important assumption is made in this definition – we assume that the system and the environment start in a product state. This is for practical reasons. However, the quantum operations formalism can even describe quantum dynamics when the system and environment do not start out in a product state.

If the principal system has a Hilbert space of $d$ dimensions, then it suffices to model the environment as being in a Hilbert space of no more than $d^2$ dimensions. It also turns out not to be necessary for the environment to start out in a mixed state; a pure state will do.

We can generalize the definition by defining quantum operations as the class of maps which arise as a result of the following processes: some initial system is prepared in an unknown quantum state $\rho$, and then brought into contact with other systems prepared in standard states, allowed to interact according to some unitary interaction, and then some part of the combined system is discarded, leaving just a final system in some state $\rho’$. The quantum operation $\mathcal{E}$ defining this process simply maps $\rho$ to $\rho’$.
